what country invented koshary?

Koshari is thought to have its roots in India and dates to the era of British colonisation. Actually, the word "Koshari" is derived from the Hindu word "khichri," which designates a rice and lentil dish. This dish was cheap and filling, and it was brought to Egypt by the British when they arrived in the late 1800s.
